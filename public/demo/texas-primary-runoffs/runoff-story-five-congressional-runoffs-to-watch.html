Five Congressional Runoffs We’re Watching From Dallas to the Border

By Chris Okonkwo · March 27, 2026 · 8 min read

Texas voters are deciding several competitive runoff elections for the U.S. House, including open-seat races in Dallas, Harris, and Bexar counties where new district lines and retirements reshaped the playing field. National party committees have poured money into a handful of contests that could affect the balance of power in Washington after November.

Our reporters are publishing candidate guides for each congressional runoff we are tracking. Explore the interactive race view below, then visit our U.S. Congress runoff index for links to every guide.

South and North Texas

In South Texas, two candidates are still competing for party nominations in districts that stretch from Laredo to the Rio Grande Valley. In North Texas, suburban turnout in Plano and Garland could decide a Dallas-area seat that national analysts rate as a general-election battleground.
