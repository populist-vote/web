Party chairs

County Chair Runoffs Shape Grassroots Infrastructure for November

By Jordan Ellis · March 27, 2026 · 7 min read

While most voters focus on candidates for public office, Texas’s major parties are also choosing county chairs—the officials who recruit precinct chairs, run county conventions, and coordinate volunteer programs that power general-election turnout. In several large counties, no candidate cleared fifty percent in March, forcing May runoffs for both Republican and Democratic county chair positions.

Party insiders say the outcomes will affect how aggressively each county party contests local races, how delegate slates are assembled for state conventions, and how ballot-security and get-out-the-vote programs are funded.
