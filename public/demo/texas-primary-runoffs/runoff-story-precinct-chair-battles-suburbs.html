Precinct chairs

Precinct Chair Battles Heat Up in Suburban Houston and North Texas

By Emily Foster · March 27, 2026 · 6 min read

Precinct chairs are the smallest elected office in Texas party politics: one per voting precinct, per party, responsible for organizing neighbors, distributing sample ballots, and in some cases influencing who becomes a delegate to state and national conventions. Runoffs for precinct chair are easy to overlook, but activists say they matter in fast-growing suburbs where new housing developments shift turnout patterns every cycle.

In Fort Bend and Harris counties, multiple precinct-level runoffs pit establishment-backed organizers against insurgent slates that emphasize different approaches to school-board coordination and legislative endorsements.
