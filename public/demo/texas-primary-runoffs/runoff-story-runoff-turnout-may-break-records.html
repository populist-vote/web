Runoff Turnout May Break Records in Texas’s Largest Counties

By Jordan Ellis · March 27, 2026 · 6 min read

County election administrators across Texas are preparing for runoff turnout that could rival presidential-year early voting in the state’s largest urban counties. With competitive races for Congress, the Legislature, and several statewide offices still unsettled after the March primary, both parties are investing in field programs aimed at voters who skipped the first round.

In Bexar and Harris counties, clerks added evening and weekend hours at high-traffic early-voting locations. Dallas County officials said they would monitor wait times and open overflow check-in lines at the American Airlines Center if lines exceed 45 minutes.

What the data shows

Through the first three days of early voting, combined turnout in the five counties we are tracking in this hub—Bexar, Fort Bend, Travis, Harris, and Dallas—was up 12 percent compared with the last comparable midterm runoff cycle. Analysts caution that comparing cycles is imperfect because district lines and the slate of races have changed, but the trend lines have caught the attention of both state parties.
