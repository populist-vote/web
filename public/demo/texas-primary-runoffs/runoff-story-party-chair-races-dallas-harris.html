Party Chair Races in Dallas and Harris Counties Draw Outside Spending

By Chris Okonkwo · March 26, 2026 · 8 min read

County chair elections rarely make television news, but in Texas’s two largest Democratic counties and in competitive Republican county organizations, independent expenditure reports show donors taking sides in May runoffs. Consultants familiar with both races say the stakes include control of party bank accounts, hiring decisions for executive directors, and relationships with statewide campaigns.

Harris County’s Democratic and Republican county chair runoffs have each attracted six-figure combined spending from PACs and individual donors, according to filings reviewed by The Daily Populist. Dallas County’s GOP county chair contest has similarly drawn mail and digital ads comparing the candidates’ records running prior conventions.
